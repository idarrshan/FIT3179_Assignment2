Interest Rates in Malaysia

This graph illustrates the monthly interest rates reported by Bank Negara Malaysia from 2014 to 2024. The lines represent different types of interest rates such as Fixed Deposit Rates for various terms, Base Lending Rates (BLR), and Savings Deposit Rates. Key events that influenced these rates are visible, particularly around 2020 during the COVID-19 pandemic, where many lending and deposit rates experienced significant drops. This trend is likely due to efforts by the central bank to provide economic stimulus during the crisis. The gradual rise in rates post-2022 reflects the global trend of monetary tightening as countries recover from the pandemic and face inflationary pressures. For example, Fixed Deposit Rates (12 months) show a consistent upward trend from mid-2022 onwards. The Base Rate (BR) and BLR similarly experienced a sharp increase after mid-2022, indicating a rise in lending costs for consumers. This graph provides a comprehensive view of the fluctuations in Malaysia's interest rates and helps users understand how economic factors and central bank policies influence these rates over time.
